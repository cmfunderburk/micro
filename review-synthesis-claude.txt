  On Rubinstein: The Path Forward

  The key reference is Binmore, Rubinstein, and Wolinsky (1986) - they showed that for general preferences, alternating offers converges to an asymmetric Nash bargaining solution as discount factors approach 1. The bargaining power weights derive from patience:

  α₁ = ln(δ₁) / (ln(δ₁) + ln(δ₂))

  So the theoretically correct approach would be:
  1. Compute patience-derived bargaining weights
  2. Solve the asymmetric Nash problem: max (u₁-d₁)^α₁ (u₂-d₂)^α₂

  This is actually cleaner than the current implementation and has solid theoretical grounding.
